Research

Dynamic Pricing in Systems with Reusable Resources

 

We investigate the revenue management in systems with reusable resources (e.g. shared vehicle, item sharing etc.) through dynamic pricing. In particular, the time-varying demand with uncertainty in the network is considered. The objective of the service provider is to match the demand with lowest total allocation cost and lost sales. We propose a robust optimization model and further seek an analytical solution.

A Continuous Model for Electric Vehicle Sharing with Battery Degradation

 

We propose an integrated model for EV sharing systems design in a continuous space. This model aims to optimize the system design considering the joint impact of battery charging and degradation, hence provides decision support on the charge station deployment, fleet balancing operations, and the battery charging and replacing policy. An analytical solution is developed using optimal transport theory, which provides fruitful managerial insights. The Tenth Triennial Symposium on Transportation Analysis (TRISTAN) (with Feng Ju and Xin Wang)

Optimizing Multi-core CPU Scheduling

 

The Operating System(OS) is an important piece of software present in virtually all computer systems, which manages computer hardware and softwares and provides common resources for computer programs. We present a MIQP model in this report to solve processes scheduling problem in multicore CPU and further propose a more general formulation using rubust optimization when the processes have random arrival time and duration. A Stochastic Multi-Agent Transportation Network Model for Evacuation Planning

 

Efficient evacuation planning gains more and more popularity in researchers because of the frequently happened disaster recently.
